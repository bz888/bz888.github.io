Neuroplasticity

updated 24/9/2021

What is Neuroplasticity

Neuroplasiticty can be viewed as a general term for the brain’s ability to modify, change and adapt (both structure and function wise in response to the individual’s experiences). The brain does this by creating/strengthening connections between neurons inside our brain.

    There are many benefits to neuroplasticity
  • Learning new things
  • Enhancing cognitive capabilites
  • Recover from strokes or traumatic brain injuries
  • Improvments towards overall brain fitness

After watching Carol Dweck’s TED talk on growth mindset, I have to realise that my attitude towards learning/improving will impact and dictate the result of my neural connections. In order to strengthen aspects of my brain I must repeatedly exercise the uncomfortable (to obtain comfort).


Ways to increase your neuroplasticity

Much like other muscle groups, the brain can be strengthened through similar methods, practise. When an activity is repeated and practised, the brain will create/strengthen the neural networks in accordance to the activity, allowing mastery towards the activity.


Growth Mindset

Growth mindset describes the underlying beliefs people have about learning and intelligence. When an individual believes they can become smarter/more proficient in their craft by investing more effort. Therefore, the extra time and effort invested results in higher achievements.
In contrast, “fixed” mindset describes the belief; talents and traits are set from birth and will not/can not be changed regardless of effort.

During this exploration, I’ve come to realise/understand the importance of mindset, and how it will affect my ability to learn. After watching Carol Dweck’s TED talk, I came to realise how I can integrate this mindset into my own learning routine; tackle challenges, don’t be phased by them, effort, effort ,effort and failure can only mean a step closer to success.
